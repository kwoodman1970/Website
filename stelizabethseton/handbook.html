St. Elizabeth Seton School

Description

A. Boundaries & Registration

St. Elizabeth Seton School is a Kindergarten to Grade 9 school, operating under the auspices of the Calgary Roman Catholic School District. Our school serves Catholic students living in the communities of Hidden Valley, Hanson Ranch and Panorama Hills.

B. Our Patron Saint

The school is named in honour of St. Elizabeth Seton. St Elizabeth Seton was the first American born saint (born in New York City). St. Elizabeth was married at age nineteen and became the mother to five children. After the death of her husband, she became a Roman Catholic and began to serve the poor in the Baltimore area, especially in the field of education. A well educated woman, she trained teachers, wrote textbooks and translated spiritual writings from French. She founded the Sisters of Charity and is considered a founder of Catholic parochial school education in the United States.

Alberta Learning establishes the program of studies and recommended time allotments per subject. A detailed outline of grade level curricula may be obtained from the school.

D. Attendance

The School Act states that students must be punctual and attend school regularly. In the event of any absence, parents are requested to contact the school to indicate the reason. This may be done by phoning the school at 374-0406. In the case of a medical or dental appointment, a note should be sent in advance.

When students are absent without such prior notice, the office will attempt to contact a parent by telephone to confirm the absence. This phone contact is necessary to ensure the safety of students and to maintain a proper record of absences.

Prolonged absence or irregular attendance results in a lack of program continuity and is detrimental to learning. As a result, students who are repeatedly late or absent without a reasonable excuse are considered to be in violation of the School Act. After five unexcused absences, the student may be referred to the school district social worker.

E. Entrance/Exit Door

We ask that students NOT use the main entrance unless they are late and are reporting their arrival to the secretary or are accompanied by a parent. Students must otherwise use their assigned door and arrange to meet siblings or friends on the tarmac at the back of our school.

F. School Parking Lot

In the interest of safety, the school parking lot is OUT OF BOUNDS at all times. Students must use the sidewalks on the perimeter of the parking lot when moving to the bus stop. Parents dropping off or picking up students must do so in the designated zones directly in front of the school. Your close attention to the signs will help to ensure that you are not ticketed and that your children are safe.

G. Visitors To The School

Parents and involved community members are welcome at St. Elizabeth School. In order to maintain a safe environment, all visitors and parent volunteers are required to enter by the front doors and register at the main office.

Also, please note the following:

  • Teachers are not to take class time to discuss individual matters with visitors.
  • Students from other schools are NOT allowed access to St. Elizabeth School unless they are part of an authorised program and accompanied by a teacher or other responsible adult.

H. Lunch Program

A supervised lunch period is provided for students. A supervision fee is charged in order to provide adequate supervision. A canteen which sells nutritional items is available on a daily basis. Students may leave the gym when they are dismissed by a supervisor to go outdoors. All students must vacate the gym by 11:55 pm to allow for clean up. Students, who remain at school for lunch, are encouraged to participate in the activities offered. Individuals not involved in some organised activity are expected to go outside. Elementary students must stay on school grounds when they eat lunch at school. It is hoped that Jr. High students will remain on school grounds also. The school cannot assume responsibility for those students who choose to leave school grounds during the lunch hour. Furthermore, when junior high students leave the grounds, it is assumed that the parents are aware and have given their child permission to do so. Failure of students to respect the noon hour expectations may result in this privilege being withdrawn for a specified period of time.

J. Lockers

All grade 7, 8 & 9 students are assigned lockers. Each locker is to be secured by a combination lock, which is provided by the student at the beginning of the school year. Students are entirely responsible for the security of their own lockers and combinations.

  • Locker combinations MUST be kept private.
  • Lock combinations MUST be provided to the homeroom teacher.
  • The lockers are the property of the school. School administration reserves the right to inspect the contents of the lockers.

NB: Valuables should not be brought to school or kept in lockers. The school will not be responsible for lost or stolen items.

K. Personal Items

Students are not to bring to school, personal items (i.e. radios, skateboards, discmans, Ipods, MP3 players, gameboys and other electronic devices, etc.) that are not directly related to their schoolwork. In addition to being distractions and/or hazards, the security of these items cannot be guaranteed. Please be aware that the school will take no responsibility for their safekeeping. Students are also discouraged from carrying any substantial amount of money.

Please have personal items marked with the student's name. Clothing and other personal items do get misplaced and can be retrieved from the two lost and found boxes located in the hallways. Students and parents are encouraged to look through these boxes, as every year excellent articles are left unclaimed. Unclaimed items will be donated to charity at Christmas, Easter and at year end.

L. Student Dress

Among the values taught in our Catholic Schools are the virtues of decency, modesty and respect. Our goal is to provide a positive and safe learning environment that will reflect a Catholic Community of Caring. It is therefore in the best interest of our school community to have a dress code that will help students to make appropriate choices regarding dress.

Students must wear clothing that is appropriate for educational activities and that will not cause a disruption to the educational process.

Specifically:

  1. Clothing or accessories must not depict slogans or advertising which is controversial, obscene, offensive and/or related to alcohol, drugs, sex, violence or inappropriate language.
  2. Clothing that reveals bare backs, bare shoulders (including spaghetti straps, bare midriffs and/or plunging necklines, tank tops, muscle shirts and fish nets) is prohibited.
  3. Any clothing, jewellery or accessories which create a safety or health concern, or, cause or threaten to cause disruption to the educational process, is prohibited.
  4. Hats or other headwear must not be worn within the school.
  5. Gang related clothing is prohibited.
  6. Outerwear (i.e. coats, jackets etc.) sunglasses, hats or headgear may not be worn indoors.
  7. Shorts and skirts must be hemmed, in good repair, and of appropriate length (reach at least to the end of the finger tips when arms are at side).

Students who arrive at school dressed inappropriately will be asked to change. It will be viewed as defiant behaviour if the student repeatedly violates the dress code on a given day or over a period of time. Consequences will range from a parent conference to a suspension.

M. Physical Education Program

Physical Education is a mandated subject and all students are required to participate unless formally excused for medical or disability reasons. If a student is not able to participate in more than two consecutive classes, a medical certificate may be required. This doctor’s note will identify the type of activity that must be avoided and the time period during which the student is excused from class participation.

N. Physical Education Dress

In order to foster a proper learning environment, promote safety, comfort, hygiene, and neatness in appearance, physical education attire is compulsory for all athletic activities in Grades 7, 8 & 9. All Junior High students must have the required St. Elizabeth Seton School outfit, consisting of shorts and a T-shirt, which are available from the school. Running shoes are also compulsory.

O. Use Of Phones

Students may use the phone in case of emergency or illness. Students will not be allowed to use the phones for personal conversations. Please ensure that all after school arrangements have been made prior to sending your child to school that day. We cannot deliver messages to children during the school day as these requests disrupt instruction and office routines. Exceptions are made for emergencies.

Cell phones are not permitted in the classroom. Students are expected to turn off their cell phones and leave them in their lockers before going to class.

P. Text Books/Band Instruments

All students are expected to take proper care of their instruments and textbooks. Students who damage or lose textbooks and/or district owned instruments are required to pay the repair or replacement cost before a replacement is issued.

Q. Permission To Leave The School

Students, who leave the building during school hours for reasons of illness, dental appointments, etc., MUST obtain permission from the office and sign out. Students who are ill must contact a parent and be picked up by a designated adult before he / she is given permission to leave. Please contact the school, in advance, whenever your child must leave for an appointment. Parents must provide the school with current phone numbers and emergency contact names and phone numbers. In case of serious injury or illness, the school may request Emergency Medical Services. The cost for these services is billed to the parents.

R. Field Trips

Field trips are a part of the curricular offerings of the school. Parents are notified of any trips as the need arises. In some instances, parents are asked to contribute a fee to cover some of the costs (bussing, entrance fee, etc.). In transporting teams to sporting events, volunteer drivers are necessary in order for the activities to occur. All parents volunteering to drive students must fill out a Volunteer Driving Form, available at the office.

S. Transportation

Charter bus service is provided to elementary students living 1.2 km from school. A fee for this service will be collected in September. Junior High School students living 2.4 km from the school are eligible to purchase a monthly Calgary Transit bus pass at a reduced rate.

Parents are cautioned that inappropriate student behaviour while travelling on school buses and/or Calgary transit can result in withdrawal of bussing privileges.

T. School Patrol

Patrols start their regular duties at the beginning of the school year. All students are expected to obey our patrols. Members of the patrol units are grade five and six students who are given an opportunity to develop and practice leadership skills. They strive to model safe and courteous traffic safety behaviour, in order to provide students a safe passage to and from school.

U. Student Fees

All students are required to pay school fees at the beginning of the year. These fees are used to pay for general supplies that your child will use throughout the school year. Extended fees are collected at the same time to pay for supplies and activities that support certain programs that your child may or may not be participating in. A detailed fee schedule is approved each year by our School Council. This fee schedule is available at the office.

School Services

A. Library

Our library holds many important resources for learning and reading enjoyment. In addition to books, the library offers computerised research stations with Internet access.

Notices are given to students with overdue books. Once the materials are overdue for more than three weeks, a bill will be sent to the parents. If the item is lost the book may be paid for or replaced with a book in good condition and of equal value.

B. Computer Lab

This facility is open to students during regular school hours and on specific lunch breaks. Internet access is also available at a large number of stations. All students and parents must sign and abide by the conditions outlined on the Internet user form before students are allowed to access this resource. Misuse of the Internet will result in serious penalty including access privileges being revoked. Students are not allowed to access chat lines including MSN messenger on school computers. Please read the permission form very carefully in order to avoid any compromising situations.

C. Counsellor

There is a Counsellor-teacher available to students. The main objectives of the Counselling program are to promote self- awareness, to encourage effective problem solving practices, and to develop effective decision making strategies. The Counsellor works in three capacities:

  • Counselling - individually, in small groups and in classes.
  • Consulting - with students, teachers, parents, agencies, etc.
  • Co-ordinating - group sessions, etc.

D. Health Services

It is important that children are well enough to learn when they come to school. Sick children should be kept at home for their own well being, and for the health of others. Students who are too ill to go outside should be kept at home. As short-term illnesses affect all children at some point, it is necessary for parents to have emergency child-care plans in place. When students come to school, they are expected to be well enough to take part in all aspects of the day including Physical Education and recess.

A community Health Nurse visits the school once a week. If you wish to refer your child to the nurse, please inform office staff. The Community Health Nurse is available for consultation and co-ordinates the planning and presentation of the health program.

E. School Council

An advisory council consisting of parents and teachers exists for the purpose of enhancing communication between the school and the community. All parents are members of this council and are invited to attend meetings. Items can be placed on the agenda by contacting the Chairman of the Council or the Principal. Meeting dates can be obtained by calling the school.

H. Agenda Book

Students are required to purchase an agenda book from the school and are expected to use it on a daily basis. Parents are encouraged to review this agenda book regularly to familiarise themselves with their child's activities. This book can be used as a daily communication tool between home and school. Note that the book is also used by grade 7, 8 & 9 students as a hall pass and must be carried when a student is out of class during instructional time. If lost the agenda must be replaced. The cost for replacement will be charged to the student.

Academic Program

A. Course Outline

Teachers provide students with course outlines at the start of the year. Included in these outlines are homework expectations and course evaluation.

B. Homework Policy

Homework is an integral part of the educational process, which reinforces classroom learning and provides for practice of new skills, and application of knowledge. Regular homework completion is essential for academic success! Parental involvement is critical to the homework process. Parents must check homework assignments for completeness and quality of content on a regular basis.

Homework is of two types:

  1. Work not completed during class time.
  2. Additional work assigned as practice or study.

Suggested activities when nothing specific is assigned:

  1. creative writing
  2. math facts
  3. subject review/study
  4. reading for comprehension

Academic and Spirit awards are presented to deserving students at the end of each reporting period. The criteria for the various awards are as follow:

Academic Awards

Per Term:
Honour Roll with Distinction

Students must have a combined average of at least 90% in Language Arts, Math, Science, Social Studies and Religion, with no mark below 80% and no NI mark in any subject listed on the report card.

Honour Roll

Students must have a combined average of at least 80% in Language Arts, Math, Science, Social Studies and Religion, with no mark below 75% and no NI mark in any subject listed on the report card.

Achievement Roll

Students must have a combined average of at least 75% in Language Arts, Math, Science, Social Studies and Religion, with no mark below 70% and no NI mark in any subject listed on the report card.

Terms 3 & 4 (only)
Plus 5 Award

A student’s overall average has increased by at least 5 percent from last term.

School Spirit Awards

Team Awards

Most Valuable Player and Most Improved Player for each team that played during that term.

Final Term
Participation

Students who have shown strong participation in areas such as school spirit, student council, yearbook, and other extracurricular activities.

(Knights of Columbus)

A Knights of Columbus committee will assess the applications, and declare a winner or winners.

An extensive music/band program is available to students. Elementary students are introduced to simple percussion instruments and choral music. Junior High students are provided with an opportunity to take part in a band instrumental program. All students who use district owned instruments are required to pay a $100.00 fee established by the school district. This fee applies to all schools throughout the Calgary Catholic District.

Co-Curricular and Extra Curricular Activities

A. Religious Activities

A number of liturgies and celebrations are presented throughout the year. These are prepared and presented by students and teachers in order to enhance and highlight the church’s liturgical calendar. On occasion, Father Andrew from St. John the Evangelist Parish is available to actively participate in these liturgies. Parents are always invited to attend these liturgies. Please watch for dates and times as communicated in monthly newsletters.

B. Student Council

The Student Council is a way for grade 7, 8 and 9 students to develop leadership skills through learning, planning and organising activities with other peers. This important and necessary Council is comprised of self-nominated grade junior high students. This Council plans and assists with social events such as student dances, fund raisers, special days and promotes school spirit and pride.

C. Inter-School Sports

St. Elizabeth Seton School enters teams in the Calgary Catholic Junior High School Athletic Associations' sponsored events. There are opportunities for junior high students to participate in most events.

These teams involve students who have the interest and ability to compete at a higher level than that which exists in the intramural program. To be eligible for such teams, a student must maintain a satisfactory record of academic achievement, classroom conduct, co-operation and contribute positively to the school community.

Our school teams are eligible to participate in cross-country running, basketball, volleyball, badminton, dance and wrestling.

D. School Dances

School dances are held from 3:15 to 4:45 p.m. These dances are hosted by Student Council. As with all school-sponsored activities, dances are only open to current St. Elizabeth Seton students – no invited guests are allowed. Permission to attend dances or extra-curricular activities may be withheld due to academic or behavioural concerns. Students leaving the dance will not be permitted to return.

Student Conduct

A. Behavioural Expectations

Students are expected to demonstrate behaviours which reflect the values of Catholic Education and are necessary for the maintenance of an effective learning-teaching environment. Any school-sponsored activity demands that students exhibit both respect and responsibility. These activities are over and above the regular school program and, as such, are privileges – not rights. Participation in these activities may be withheld due to breaches of the school's behaviour code.

Specifically, a student is expected to:

  1. Show respect for the rights and dignity of others. Harassment, either verbal or physical is considered a violation of this expectation. Vulgar or obscene language, rude or insolent comments or racial taunts are not acceptable.
  2. Show respect for property – his/her own and that of others including the school building, equipment and grounds. Graffiti, vandalism and theft are considered violations of these expectations.
  3. Show respect for him/herself. A student is expected to be prepared for class and to work consistently to the level of his/her ability. The use of tobacco, drugs or alcohol on school property or during school hours is also considered to be a violation of this expectation.

B. School Progressive Discipline Plan

The maintenance of discipline in school is the responsibility of the teachers, staff and school administration.

Specific disciplinary measures used at St. Elizabeth Seton School include the following:

  1. Warning/discussion with student regarding the concern.
  2. Time Out
  3. Informal/formal talks between student, teacher, counsellor, parents, administration
  4. Detention
  5. Community service
  6. In-school suspension – In such instances, parents are contacted
  7. Formal suspension ranging from 1-5 days
    1. parents and Superintendent notified in writing
    2. parents must meet with administration to discuss conditions of reinstatement and corrective measures.
  8. Recommendation for expulsion.

The seriousness of an offence may be such that the school resorts directly to formal suspension.

Noon hour or after school detentions are assigned by teachers for violations of school and classroom regulations (incomplete assignments, tardiness, disruptive behaviour in class).

We are proud of the conduct demonstrated by the majority of our students and for the small minority of students who have difficulty in this regard, we believe positive change will result from:

  1. continued communication and co-operation between home and school;
  2. application of the disciplinary measures noted above.
